About

History Background:
Bellevue College, located in Bellevue, Washington, was established in
1966 as a community college. Originally known as Bellevue Community
College, the institution was formed to provide accessible and affordable
higher education to the growing Eastside community. Over the years, Bellevue College
has grown in both size and scope, offering a wide range of academic
programs and serving as a hub for innovation and creativity in the Pacific Northwest.

Our Values:
Bellevue College values accessibility, diversity, excellence, innovation, integrity, and learning. These core values guide the institution's mission to provide an inclusive, student-centered learning environment that promotes academic and personal growth.

Our President:
Gary Locke values education, diversity, and equity. He is committed to promoting student success and ensuring that every student has access to affordable, high-quality education. He also advocates for social justice and inclusion in all areas of society.
